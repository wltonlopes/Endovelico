# Syracuse

## História

Syracuse, founded on the coast of the island of Sicily, grew to be one of the largest Greek cities in the Meditrranean basin, rivaling Athens in size by the end of the 5th century BC. Its storied history includes internal revolutions and external wars against emerging Mediterranean powers such as Carthage, Athens, and Rome, with a brief stint as part of the empire of Pyrrhus of Epirus and for a while as master of most of Sicily. Quinquiremes were invented there, as well as numerous siege engine innovations, due to the patronage of the city's famous tyrants.
