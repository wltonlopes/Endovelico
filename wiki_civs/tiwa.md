# Pukara

## História
The Pukara civilization (c. 1400 BCE – 400 CE), based in the northern Lake Titicaca Basin of Peru, was a pioneering Andean culture that established an early, complex state-level society. It peaked around 200 BCE – 200 CE, known for monumental red-stone architecture, sophisticated ceramics, and agricultural innovation, acting as a direct precursor to the Tiwanaku culture.
